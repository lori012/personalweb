About Me

I am a third-year undergraduate in physics at the University of Louisville in Louisville, Kentucky. I'm currently involved in research under Dr. Benne Holwerda at UofL, and Drs. Blakesley Burkhart and Matt Orr at the Center for Computational Astrophysics. I graduated high school in May 2019 from the Craft Academy for Excellence in Science and Mathematics.

I am originally from Campbellsburg, Kentucky, a rural town where I first fell in love with the stars. Keeping this admiration in mind, I headed to high school at iLead Academy. in Carrollton, KY. Far from the traditional high school experience, iLead is a selective high school program that gave me the opportunity to explore more into STEM.

Starting my junior year of high school, I switched paths from aerospace engineering to astronomy and astrophysics, and attended the Craft Academy for Excellence in Science and Mathematics. Craft is a highly selective, residential, dual-credit high school on the campus of Morehead State University. I found Craft to be much more suited to my interests, and gained a head start on my undergraduate degree by obtaining 60+ hours in courses by my high school graduation. Here, I found a love for outreach and advocacy. I also took my first steps into undergraduate research.

I knew for a fact that I wanted to continue studying astronomy and astrophysics. This realization led me to enroll at the University of Louisville for my undergraduate degree. I am currently at UofL on a full-ride scholarship supported by the Brown Fellows Program, a fellowship that pays for both my schooling and for two enrichment projects, taking place the summers after my sophomore and junior years.

Currently, my interests revolve around my research projects and extracurricular activities. I am a member of several campus organizations, including the Society for Women In Physics and Astronomy, Mortar Board, and an officer position in UofL's Society for Physics Students chapter. Here, I found a love for advocacy of science, minority involvement, and gifted education.

My hobbies outside of academia include hiking, camping, kayaking, travelling, baking, cooking, thrift shopping, and taking care of my many houseplants. In my free time you can usually find me doing one of the above activities or attending a concert or music festival.
